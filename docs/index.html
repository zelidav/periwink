Our story

She thought she was the only one.

Every woman's journey through this chapter begins with a quiet moment of recognition.

1

She's navigating something no one talks about.

The hot flashes at 3am. The brain fog in meetings. The sudden anxiety that has no name. She Googles symptoms alone at midnight, wondering if this is just her.

2

She starts to wonder: is anyone else feeling this?

The doctor visits feel rushed. Friends change the subject. The wellness industry offers solutions but not understanding. She doesn't need another product. She needs to feel seen.

3

She finds a place that finally gets it.

A space where "me too" isn't a hashtag — it's a lifeline. Where women share what's actually working. Where tracking her symptoms reveals patterns she never noticed. Where she can be anonymous or known, on her terms.

4

She found her people.

And together, they're not just getting through it — they're redefining what this chapter means. More wisdom. More honesty. More of the conversations that should have been happening all along.

What we're building

Four pillars of a different kind of community

Every feature exists because a woman like you said she needed it.

💬

Community Rooms

Topic-based circles for the conversations that matter most. Post under your pseudonym or go fully anonymous. Threaded discussions where lived experience is valued as much as expertise.

📖

Symptom Tracker

Daily logging across 25 symptoms — vasomotor, sleep, mood, physical, and more. Rate severity, note patterns, and finally see the shape of what your body has been telling you.

🔬

Citizen Science

Opt in to contribute your anonymous data to community insights. See what symptoms are most common, which treatments are helping, and discover correlations no one is studying yet.

🔒

Privacy First

Pseudonyms by default. Full anonymity per-post. Granular consent controls over every piece of data. Your story is yours — you decide who sees what, always.

# Periwink — User Guide
### A calm, intelligent community for women navigating perimenopause and menopause

---

## What Is Periwink?

Periwink is a private, supportive community where women going through perimenopause and menopause can connect, share experiences, track symptoms, and feel less alone. Think of it as a warm, thoughtful space built by women, for women — where everything you share is on your terms.

---

## Getting Started

### Visiting the Site

Go to **www.yourperiwink.com** in your phone or computer browser.

You'll see a beautiful landing page with a flowing botanical video. From here you can:

- **Tap "Join"** to see the butterfly welcome experience
- **Scroll down** to read about the community, hear from other women, and learn about the founder
- **Sign up** for the community or apply to be a founding member

### Adding Periwink to Your Phone

You can save Periwink to your home screen so it opens like a regular app — no app store needed.

**On iPhone:**
1. Open www.yourperiwink.com in **Safari** (it has to be Safari)
2. Tap the **Share button** (the square with the arrow pointing up)
3. Scroll down and tap **"Add to Home Screen"**
4. Tap **Add**

**On Android:**
1. Open www.yourperiwink.com in **Chrome**
2. Tap the **three dots** menu in the top right
3. Tap **"Add to Home Screen"** or **"Install App"**

Now you'll have a Periwink icon on your home screen that opens full-screen.

## Signing Up

### Community Signup

The community signup asks for three things:

- **Your name** — how you'd like to be addressed (first name is fine)
- **Your email** — so we can reach you
- **A pseudonym** — this is your identity in the community. Pick anything you like. You can be as anonymous as you want. Other members only see your pseudonym, never your real name or email.

### Founding Member Application

If you want to help shape what Periwink becomes, you can apply as a founding member. This is for healthcare practitioners, researchers, content creators, community builders, and brand partners who want to be involved early.

The application asks about your background and what you'd bring to the community. Adrian reviews every application personally.

## The Community App

Once you have an account, you can explore the full Periwink community at the app URL.

### Home Feed

Your home feed shows recent posts from across the community. You'll see what women are sharing, what's resonating, and what conversations are happening.

### Rooms

Rooms are focused discussion spaces organized by topic. Some examples:

- Hot Flashes & Night Sweats
- Sleep & Fatigue  
- HRT & Hormone Therapy
- Relationships & Intimacy
- Brain Fog & Memory
- Mood & Emotions
- Nutrition & Movement
- Identity & Life Changes

You automatically follow some rooms when you join. You can follow or unfollow any room.

### Posting

When you write a post, you choose:

- **Which room** it belongs in
- **Your identity** — post as your pseudonym, or go fully anonymous

There's no judgment here. Say what you need to say.

### Reacting

You can react to posts and comments with:

- **Heart** — love and appreciation
- **Hug** — warmth and support
- **Same** — "I feel this too"
- **Helpful** — useful information

These reactions are the community's way of saying "I see you" without needing words.

### Stories

The Stories section features especially meaningful posts that have been highlighted by the community or moderators. These are posts that capture something real.

### Check-In

The check-in is a guided way to log how you're feeling. You can track symptoms like:

- Hot flashes, night sweats, sleep issues
- Brain fog, mood changes, anxiety
- Joint pain, fatigue, weight changes
- And many more

You rate severity from 1-5 and can add notes. Over time, this builds a personal picture of your experience.

### Profile

Your profile shows your pseudonym and whatever you choose to share. Everything is optional. You control what's visible to others.

## Privacy

Privacy is built into everything about Periwink.

- **Your real name and email are never shown** to other members
- **Your pseudonym** is all anyone sees
- **Anonymous posting** is always available — even your pseudonym is hidden
- **Symptom data** is yours. If you choose to contribute to community insights (citizen science), the data is anonymized and aggregated — no one can trace it back to you
- **You can opt in or out** of data sharing at any time
